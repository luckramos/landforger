The Ninth Vale is older than the guild's numbering scheme for eras, older than [[duskwater]]'s foundations, older, some of the guild's more superstitious apprentices insist, than the tide itself. It does not belong to any Era on the timeline because it was never founded, conquered, or renamed on anyone's schedule — it was simply here, is here, and by every indication will go on being here after the current argument about who owns the coast is settled or drowned.

That permanence is exactly why the vale matters to the [[cartographers-guild]]'s work: every other feature on the coast — [[ashthorn-keep]], the [[harbor-gate]], even [[duskwater]] itself — can be dated against it, because the vale never moves and never changes hands. [[sera]] uses it as the one fixed point on every chart she draws.

It is also, less comfortably, where the [[hollow-king]] is most often seen, and where [[corin]] has taken to walking when he has nowhere else he'll admit to going.

No guild record credits a builder for the Highland Stair; the steps are older than the [[cartographers-guild]]'s oldest chart of the [[ninth-vale]], cut into stone that doesn't match any quarry within three days' walk. It climbs a little over four hundred steps from the vale floor to a shelf of high ground that has never, in any account [[sera]] has been able to verify, gone under water.

That single fact makes it one of the few reliable survey markers left on the coast. Sera re-anchors her charts from the top of the stair every season, and half the guild's flood-year predictions trace back, one way or another, to a measurement taken from these steps.

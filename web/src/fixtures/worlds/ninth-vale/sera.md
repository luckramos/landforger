## The last honest chart of the coast

Sera Valen was born in the flood year they still argue about the name of, in a room above the fish-salters' hall in [[duskwater]] that has since gone under twice. She apprenticed to the [[cartographers-guild]] at eleven, not because she loved maps but because the guild hall was the only building in the district with a roof that didn't leak, and by nineteen she had redrawn the harbor twice — once for the guild, once for herself, in the margins, in the places she was told the water didn't reach.

It reached there anyway. That is, more or less, the whole of her professional biography: Sera keeps drawing the coast as it actually is, and the coast keeps proving her right in the worst possible way. She has walked the shoreline from [[highland-stair]] to the [[harbor-gate]] with the [[tidewane-compass]] in her coat pocket more times than the guild's ledgers admit, because most of those walks were never commissioned — they were hers, chasing a rumor that the [[the-ninth-map]] survived the [[the-sundering]] after all, hidden somewhere the [[order-ember]] hasn't thought to burn yet.

:::callout {type="info"}

Guild record, third rank examination: "Cartographer Valen's charts are accurate to a fault — she will not omit a feature to make a coastline easier to sell. This is, the examiners note, either her greatest asset or the reason she will never make second rank."

:::

What she is not, whatever [[corin]] insists after the third drink, is reckless. Sera has turned back from three expeditions the Order would have called cowardice and the Guild quietly called sense. She only pushes forward when the compass agrees with her — and lately, it has been agreeing more and more.

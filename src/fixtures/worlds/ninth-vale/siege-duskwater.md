[[ashthorn-keep]] held for three weeks against a force no surviving record names clearly — raiders, some accounts say; something worse, others insist. What every account agrees on is the ending: the gate opened from the inside, the captain-general was never seen again, and [[corin]], who commanded that gate, was exiled for it within the month.

[[duskwater]] itself was never directly assaulted, but the siege's ending is the reason the city still calls that whole stretch of the drowning years by the keep's name rather than its own. The [[cartographers-guild]] dates half its founding-era charts against the siege for lack of anything more precise.

Corin Ashthorn held the gate of [[ashthorn-keep]] for eleven years before the [[siege-duskwater]] ended with the keep's own captain-general opening a door that should have stayed shut. Corin did not open it. He has spent every year since failing to make anyone with the authority to matter believe that.

> "I have told this story to three magistrates, one bishop of the Ember, and a fisherman who happened to be standing nearby. Only the fisherman believed me, and he had no power to unmake my exile. Make of that what you will."

Now he walks the [[ninth-vale]] region on no one's authority but his own, half bodyguard and half ghost story, and has attached himself — loosely, and by her account unasked — to [[sera]]'s expeditions along the coast. He says it's penance. She says it's convenient, since he's the only person she trusts who still remembers where the old keep's foundations actually sit.

He does not speak of the [[hollow-king]] by name if he can help it. When he can't help it, he speaks of little else.

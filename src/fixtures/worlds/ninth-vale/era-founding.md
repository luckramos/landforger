No calendar the [[cartographers-guild]] keeps reaches back far enough to date the Founding Tides properly; the era is named for what came after it, not for anything that happened during it. The [[ninth-vale]] already stood. The [[highland-stair]] was already old. Whatever built the [[tidewane-compass]] had already finished and moved on, or never existed to begin with.

The Founding Tides end, by every account, on the night of [[the-sundering]] — the last night anyone could still call the coastline settled.

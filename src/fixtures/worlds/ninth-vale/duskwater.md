## A city built to be redrawn

Duskwater was founded on a slope steep enough that no single flood has ever taken the whole of it at once — which is, depending on who you ask, either bad planning turned lucky or the entire point. The [[cartographers-guild]] keeps its hall here for exactly this reason: a city that has to be re-surveyed every few years keeps a guild in permanent, profitable work.

The city divides itself, informally but absolutely, into the districts everyone actually navigates by: the [[harbor-gate]] and its ropewalks, the [[salt-market]] terraces above the tideline, the guild's own [[guild-hall]] quarter, and the low streets everyone now simply calls the [[drowned-quarter]], because half of it is.

---

[[sera]] was born here, three streets from where the [[drowned-quarter]] now starts, in a building that no longer exists in any tense but the past. She still keeps a room above the guild hall, for the nights the coast doesn't let her get home.
